About

I am a second year student pursuing my Masters in Computer Science at the University of Southern California (USC). A Software Engineer by choice, I also enjoy working on inter-disciplinary problems with data, broadly in the fields of Machine Learning, Data Mining, Informational Retrieval and Natural Language Processing.

I spent my Summer 2018 interning at Intel Corporation in Folsom, working with the Graphics Validation team. I developed plugins in Python to enable the automation of the Graphics Test Software. I modelled the debug process collection and improved the data analysis of test results.

At USC, I worked as a Research Assistant in Information Sciences Institute under Prof. Gully Burns in the Evidence Extraction Project. I developed the Domain Insight Graphs for the underlying data and worked on dockerizing few applications.

Prior to joining USC for my Masters, I worked as an Associate Software Engineer at Robert Bosch. Working in the Car Infotainment unit, I familiarlized myself with C++ and UI Tools like EBGuide and CGI. With a background in MVC framework, I also worked in the middleware layer to provide wrappers to the driver from the application layer.

Most of my research forays have come as a part of my course projects. I have benefitted from having some great teachers who have guided successful projects. They are listed below.
